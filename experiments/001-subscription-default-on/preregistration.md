# Experiment 001: Subscription Default ON

**Status:** Pre-registered, not yet launched.
**Owner:** Frederick Fermin
**Pre-registration filed:** 2026-05-26

This document is filed before the experiment runs. Deviations from the plan after launch must be documented and justified in a separate addendum.

## Hypothesis

Defaulting the subscription option to ON on the PDP will increase the subscriber rate (share of purchases that are subscriptions) without significantly hurting overall conversion rate.

**Mechanism we believe is at play:** status quo bias. With subscription pre-selected, buyers who are indifferent between purchase types accept the default rather than actively switching. Buyers with strong preference for one-time purchase will still switch in either variant.

**Counter-hypothesis we're guarding against:** defaulting to subscription with a 15% discount may make some buyers feel manipulated, causing cart abandonment. This would hurt the conversion rate, not just shift purchases between types. The guardrail metric exists to catch this.

## Variants

| Variant | Allocation | Behavior |
|---|---:|---|
| Control | 50% | PDP loads with "One-time purchase" radio selected |
| Treatment | 50% | PDP loads with "Subscribe & Save 15%" radio selected |

Identical UI in both arms. Only the initial default selection differs. Users can manually switch to the other option in either variant.

## Population

Visitors landing on any product page (`/products/*`) on `lumen-dev-d5fvasxb.myshopify.com`.

**Exclusions:**
- Known bots (user-agent filter applied at analysis time)
- Returning visitors already assigned to a variant on a prior session (sticky assignment via `user_pseudo_id` hash)

## Randomization

- **Unit:** `user_pseudo_id` (UUID v4 generated by the Custom Pixel and stored in the pixel sandbox's localStorage as `lumen_upid`)
- **Method:** deterministic hash of `sha256(user_pseudo_id + ":" + experiment_id) mod 2`. Stable for the same user across sessions.
- **Allocation:** 50/50

## Metrics

| Type | Metric | Definition | Direction of success |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Subscriber rate | subscription purchases / total purchases | Higher is better |
| Secondary | Revenue per visitor | total revenue / unique exposed visitors | Higher is better |
| Guardrail | Conversion rate | total purchases / unique exposed visitors | Must not drop by more than 5% relative |

Only the primary metric carries statistical significance weight for the ship decision. Secondaries are reported but not the basis for the call.

## Statistical design

**Primary test:** two-proportion z-test, two-sided.

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| α | 0.05 | Standard |
| Power (1 − β) | 0.80 | Standard |
| Baseline subscriber rate p₁ | 0.12 | Industry benchmark for DTC beverage subscription products |
| Treatment subscriber rate p₂ | 0.15 | MDE of +3 percentage points absolute |
| MDE (absolute) | 0.03 | Conservative; smaller than typical defaults-ON effects reported (5-10pp) |

**Sample size calculation:**

```
n_per_variant = ((z_{α/2} + z_β)² × (p₁(1-p₁) + p₂(1-p₂))) / (p₂-p₁)²
              = ((1.96 + 0.84)² × (0.12×0.88 + 0.15×0.85)) / 0.03²
              = (7.84 × 0.2331) / 0.0009
              ≈ 2030 purchases per variant
              ≈ 4060 total purchases
```

**Visitor volume implied:** at an assumed conversion rate of 2.5%, this requires ~160,000 total exposed visitors (~80k per variant) to achieve the necessary purchase count. The traffic simulator targets this volume.

**Guardrail test:** one-sided z-test on conversion rate.
- H₀: treatment_conv ≥ 0.95 × control_conv (a drop of at most 5% relative)
- Rejection of H₀ at α = 0.05 = guardrail violated

## Stopping rule

Fixed-horizon. No peeking.

The experiment runs until the pre-specified sample size is reached, then is analyzed exactly once.

Rationale: sequential peeking inflates the false-positive rate well beyond the nominal α. Committing to N upfront preserves the 5% guarantee. Group-sequential designs and sequential probability ratio tests exist for the case where early stopping matters; we are not using them here.

## Analysis plan

To be implemented in `experiments/001-subscription-default-on/analyze.py`. Reproducible from the data in Athena.

1. Query Athena for all events between experiment start date and (start + duration). Required event types: `product_viewed`, `product_added_to_cart`, `checkout_completed`.
2. Filter to events with `data.experiment_id = "001"` and `data.variant ∈ {"control", "treatment"}`. These fields are set by the variant-assignment JS and forwarded by the pixel.
3. For each variant, compute:
   - Total exposed visitors (distinct `user_pseudo_id` with at least one `product_viewed` event in the period)
   - Total purchases (distinct `checkout_completed` events)
   - Subscription purchases (purchases where `data.is_subscription = true`)
   - Total revenue (sum of `value` across `checkout_completed`)
4. Compute the three metrics per variant.
5. Run the three pre-specified tests. Report: point estimate of effect, 95% confidence interval, p-value, decision.
6. Visualize: subscriber rate by day, cumulative effect over the run.

## Pre-specified ship/no-ship rules

- **Ship treatment** if: primary metric significantly increases (p < 0.05, two-sided) AND guardrail is not violated.
- **Do not ship** if: primary metric does not significantly increase, OR guardrail is violated and the primary effect is not large enough to compensate.
- **Escalate to judgment** if: primary is positive but the guardrail is violated. Document the tradeoff in the analysis writeup before deciding.

## Pitfalls and mitigations

| Pitfall | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Peeking inflates false-positive rate | Fixed-horizon stopping rule, no interim looks |
| Multiple comparisons | One primary metric. Secondaries reported with explicit caveat that they do not gate the decision |
| Novelty / primacy effect | Run for at least 14 calendar days of simulated traffic to span multiple shopping cycles |
| Selection bias | Randomization at first qualifying page view. No opt-out, no late-binding |
| Survivorship bias | Denominator is all exposed visitors, not just purchasers |
| SRM (sample ratio mismatch) | At analysis time, verify the variant allocation is within tolerance (chi-square p > 0.001 on observed vs expected counts) |
| Dilution from sticky assignment failure | Variant ID is written to a parameter the pixel sends with every event. Analysis filters on the assigned variant from the first exposure |
